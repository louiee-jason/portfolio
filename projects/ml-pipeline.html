Bank Customer Segmentation & Classification

An end-to-end machine learning pipeline addressing the lack of labeled banking data by using clustering for segmentation, followed by supervised classification to predict customer risk and behavioral profiles.

Project Overview

Banks often lack labeled data to truly understand their customer behavior, making targeted marketing, risk profiling, and retention strategies incredibly difficult. This project directly solves that issue by building a complete machine learning pipeline.

First, unsupervised clustering was used to group customers into behavioral segments based on their transactional data. Once these segments were verified, supervised classification models were trained to predict these exact segments for any new incoming customers.

Phase 1: Unsupervised Learning (Clustering)

The first phase successfully segmented the bank's customers into distinct profiles:

  • Preprocessing & Engineering: Handled missing data, applied One-Hot Encoding, scaled with StandardScaler, and binned features.
  • Optimal Cluster Selection: Utilized the Elbow Method and evaluated Silhouette Scores to mathematically determine the ideal number of clusters.
  • Model Training: Trained a K-Means Clustering algorithm with PCA for dimensionality reduction.

Identified Cluster Insights:

  • Segment 0 (High-Value): High-income customers with frequent transactions.
  • Segment 1 (Low-Engagement): Low activity customers with minimal transactions.
  • Segment 2 (Potential Risk): Moderate income customers exhibiting high credit usage.

Phase 2: Supervised Learning (Classification)

With the customers accurately labeled into the 3 segments above, predictive models were built.

  • Algorithm Selection: Trained both Decision Tree and Random Forest Classifiers.
  • Hyperparameter Tuning: Conducted extensive optimization using GridSearchCV.
  • Model Evaluation: The Random Forest model achieved strong performance across Accuracy, Precision, Recall, and F1-score, proving that clustering-based labeling is highly effective for training supervised models.

Business Impact

  • Targeted Marketing: Enables specialized campaigns focused solely on the high-value (Segment 0) customers.
  • Risk Mitigation: Helps preemptively identify risky customer segments with high credit usage (Segment 2).
  • Churn Prevention: Supports strategic retention efforts specifically designed for low-engagement users (Segment 1).
