E-Commerce Data Analysis & Dashboard

An interactive Streamlit dashboard built to analyze e-commerce transaction data, uncover revenue patterns, evaluate product performance, and segment customers using RFM analysis.

Project Overview

This project focuses on analyzing an extensive e-commerce transaction dataset to uncover critical business insights related to revenue patterns, customer behavior, and product performance. The findings and metrics are delivered through an interactive, highly visual dashboard built with Streamlit and Plotly.

The project covers the entire data pipeline — from data wrangling and preprocessing to exploratory data analysis (EDA) and ultimately deploying a fully interactive web application for stakeholders to monitor key performance indicators (KPIs).

Objectives

  • Understand historical revenue trends and sales patterns over time.
  • Analyze product category performance to identify top-selling items.
  • Identify customer purchasing behaviors and payment method preferences.
  • Perform RFM (Recency, Frequency, Monetary) analysis to accurately segment customers into High, Mid, and Low value categories.

Methodology

The analysis followed a structured data science workflow:

  • Data Preprocessing: Handled missing values, removed outliers using the IQR method, transformed features, and formatted datetime columns.
  • Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA): Explored revenue trends over time, product category contributions, and payment method distributions.
  • Customer Segmentation: Applied RFM analysis to identify high-value customers and low-engagement buyers.
  • Dashboard Development: Built an interactive dashboard using Streamlit, featuring Plotly visualizations for dynamic exploration of metrics and trends.

Key Insights

  • Revenue Trends: Revenue peaked in late 2017, but the repeat purchase rate remained relatively low overall.
  • Product Dominance: A small number of product categories generate the vast majority of revenue, strongly aligning with the Pareto principle.
  • Retention Issues: Most customers only make a single transaction, indicating low customer retention that needs addressing.
  • Payment Preferences: Credit cards are overwhelmingly the dominant payment method chosen by users.

Business Recommendations

  • Implement targeted loyalty programs to significantly improve customer retention and increase purchase frequency.
  • Target one-time buyers with personalized promotions or discount campaigns.
  • Focus marketing efforts and inventory management on the highest-performing product categories.
