KRSBI Robotics Project

Contributed to the Indonesian Robot Contest (KRSBI) with embedded systems programming, hardware integration, and competition-level robot preparation.

Project Overview

The Kontes Robot Sepak Bola Indonesia (KRSBI) is one of Indonesia's most prestigious robotics competitions, challenging teams to design, build, and program robots capable of autonomous and semi-autonomous operation. Participating in this national-level competition provided invaluable hands-on experience in robotics engineering.

As a team member, I contributed to multiple aspects of the robot development process, from electronics integration and embedded systems programming to testing and competition-day troubleshooting. The experience demanded strong teamwork, technical versatility, and the ability to work under pressure.

Responsibilities & Contributions

  • Electronics Integration: Assembled and wired the robot's electronic components including motor drivers, microcontrollers, power distribution boards, and sensor modules. Ensured reliable electrical connections that could withstand the physical demands of competition.
  • Embedded Systems Programming: Developed Arduino-based firmware for robot control, including motor speed regulation, sensor data processing, and communication between multiple microcontrollers within the robot system.
  • Hardware Troubleshooting: Diagnosed and resolved hardware issues during development and testing phases, including loose connections, motor driver failures, sensor malfunctions, and power management problems.
  • Testing & Calibration: Conducted systematic testing of robot subsystems, calibrated sensors for optimal performance, and fine-tuned control parameters to achieve desired robot behavior.
  • Competition Preparation: Participated in pre-competition readiness checks, ensuring all systems were reliable and the robot met competition regulations and specifications.

Technical Details

The robot system utilized a multi-controller architecture to manage different subsystems effectively:

  • Arduino Controllers: Multiple Arduino boards managed different robot functions — motor control, sensor processing, and communication — with serial communication between boards.
  • Motor Control: H-bridge motor drivers controlled DC motors with PWM speed regulation, enabling precise movement control and direction changes.
  • Sensor Suite: The robot utilized various sensors including ultrasonic distance sensors, infrared line sensors, and encoders for position tracking and environmental awareness.
  • C++ Programming: All embedded firmware was written in C++ using the Arduino framework, with focus on efficient code execution for real-time control loops.
  • Power Management: A custom power distribution system managed battery power across all electronic components with appropriate voltage regulation and protection circuits.

Challenges & Solutions

  • Time Pressure: Competition deadlines required efficient project management and prioritization of critical systems over nice-to-have features.
  • Mechanical-Electrical Interface: Ensuring electronics were properly mounted and protected within the robot chassis required close collaboration with the mechanical team.
  • Communication Reliability: Serial communication between multiple controllers occasionally experienced data loss, resolved through error checking and protocol optimization.
  • Competition Environment: The robot needed to perform reliably in unfamiliar environments with different lighting, surface conditions, and electromagnetic interference.

Key Learnings

  • Competitive robotics engineering and systems integration
  • Arduino/embedded systems programming for real-time control
  • Team collaboration under pressure and tight deadlines
  • Hardware debugging and systematic troubleshooting methodologies
  • Multi-subsystem robot architecture design
  • Competition preparation and performance optimization
